Most contractors discover a federal opportunity when the solicitation drops. By that point the win is already shaped. Section L tells you exactly how the proposal must be written. Section M tells you exactly how it will be scored. The buyer knows what they want. You are reacting.

The win is shaped earlier. It is shaped at the **Pre-Solicitation Synopsis** — sometimes called a "Special Notice" — published 30 to 90 days before the solicitation itself.

A pre-solicitation tells you four things you cannot get from the final solicitation:

1. **The KO's mental model.** The way they describe the requirement before legal review reveals what they actually want. By the time the solicitation is written, that voice has been smoothed away.
2. **The candidate vendor field.** Pre-solicitations name the agencies the KO consulted, the technical liaisons assigned, and sometimes — if you read carefully — the contractors who participated in market research. Those are your competitors.
3. **The SOW influence window.** This is the only point in the lifecycle where you can submit a capability statement that materially shapes Section L language. After the solicitation drops, every word is locked.
4. **The schedule pressure.** If pre-sol drops 30 days before solicitation and 60 days before due — that is a different sales motion than 90 days out.

Most subs scan SAM.gov for solicitations. The disciplined ones scan for pre-sols. The disciplined ones win 2x as often, because they shape the document while it is still soft clay.
