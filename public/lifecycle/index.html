The Moat

Competitors sell discovery. FARaudit sells survival.

Every other tool ends the conversation at "you should bid this." FARaudit starts there. We are the only product that:

  • Reads the contract. Three-call Claude pipeline (Overview / Compliance / Risks) parses every PDF clause-by-clause, including the buried trap clauses (DFARS 252.223-7008 hex chrome, 252.204-7018 SCRM, 252.204-7021 CMMC) that disqualify subs at award.
  • Tells you what to ask the KO. Auto-drafts clarification questions tied to ambiguous CLINs, conflicting FOB terms, and Section L formatting traps. Sends through Resend. Tracks the response.
  • Stays with you past award. Stages 06–07 continue compliance monitoring, mod tracking, and closeout — the work that costs subs millions when neglected.

Moat compounds: every audit improves the trap-clause classifier. Govwin can't catch up by adding features — they'd have to rebuild on a contract-aware model.

Pivot: "And on the three they read carefully — they're still going to miss DFARS 252.223-7008 if it's buried in Section H paragraph 14. We've tested this. Even compliance attorneys miss trap clauses at non-trivial rates."

Acknowledge: "You're right to be skeptical. We were paranoid about this when we built it."

Boundary: "FARaudit doesn't replace your contracts attorney. It surfaces the clauses she should look at first, and gets her there in 25 seconds instead of 90 minutes."
