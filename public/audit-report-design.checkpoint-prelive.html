P0 Statement of Need not attached — technical specs unknown without site-visit attendance Deal-breaker
The 20-page solicitation PDF references an unattached Statement of Need (Attachment 1, dated 02 Apr 2026) and an Insignia and Markings document (Attachment 2). Without either, a vendor cannot determine dimensional drawings, material specifications, NSN references, or fit/form/function criteria for T-38-specific intake plugs and exhaust covers. Quoting against the solicitation body alone risks an "Unacceptable" technical rating with no recovery path under LPTA.
P0 DFARS 252.223-7008 hex-chrome supply chain unverified — rejection at destination probable Deal-breaker
DFARS 252.223-7008 Aerospace ground support hardware routinely uses chromate conversion coatings or zinc chromate primers. Failure to verify and certify all materials in the supply chain — including brackets, hardware, and fittings — will result in rejection at JBSA Randolph destination inspection and a cure notice with no relief under a firm-fixed-price purchase order.
P0 FOB inconsistency + 4-8 wk base-access lead time vs. 11-wk post-award delivery window Deal-breaker
FOB terms are internally inconsistent — CLIN 0001 is Government Destination while 0002 and 0003 are Contractor Destination. Layered on top, clause 5352.242-9000 requires contractor personnel credentials for JBSA Randolph, which typically takes 4-8 weeks to process against an ~11-week post-award delivery window. Compounded schedule-default risk.
P1 CLIN 0001 unit-of-measure ambiguity — "80 Each" vs. "SET OF 2" pricing mismatch Technical
CLIN 0001 is priced per "Each" at quantity 80, but the line-item description reads "INTAKE PLUGS (SET OF 2)". It is ambiguous whether 80 Each means 80 individual plugs or 80 sets (160 plugs total). Misinterpretation will cause either a non-conforming delivery (wrong quantity) or a grossly mispriced quote — both result in rejection or loss on an FFP order.
P1 Missing manufacturer name + part number requirements (Section L, Special Note 6) Technical
Section L instructions require quotes to include manufacturer name, part number, illustrations, and product literature to demonstrate technical acceptability against the unattached Statement of Need. Without dimensional drawings or NSN references in the solicitation body, sourcing a verifiable MFR/PN pairing before the 27 Apr 2026 quote deadline is a hard constraint.

Subject FA301626Q0068 — Pre-quote clarification questions (T-38 Talon Intake Plugs & Exhaust Covers)

Good afternoon Ms. O'Shaughnessy,

Thank you for the opportunity to quote on solicitation FA301626Q0068 for T-38 Talon intake plugs and exhaust covers in support of the 12th Maintenance Group at JBSA Randolph. Before finalizing our submission for the 27 April 2026 deadline, we would appreciate clarification on the following three items:

  1. FOB designation reconciliation (CLINs 0001 vs. 0002/0003). The CLIN structure shows FOB Government / Destination for CLIN 0001 and FOB Contractor / Destination for CLINs 0002 and 0003, while Special Instruction #7 indicates uniform FOB Destination pricing across all CLINs. Could you confirm the intended freight liability — specifically whether CLIN 0001 risk of loss during transit rests with the Government or the contractor, and whether CLIN 0003 (Shipping) is redundant given FOB Destination pricing already embedded in 0001 and 0002?
  2. CLIN 0001 unit of measure. The line-item description for CLIN 0001 reads "INTAKE PLUGS (SET OF 2)" at quantity 80 Each. Could you confirm whether 80 Each refers to 80 individual plugs, or 80 sets (160 plugs total)? This materially affects both our cost basis and the conforming delivery count.
  3. Hexavalent chromium certification path (DFARS 252.223-7008). Our supply chain analysis indicates that several candidate manufacturers for both CLINs use chromate conversion coatings on bracket and fitting hardware. Could you confirm the acceptable certification format — specifically, whether a manufacturer's letter stating compliance is sufficient, or whether MIL-STD-171F-style finishing process documentation is required at quote submission versus at delivery?

We would also appreciate confirmation that the Statement of Need (Attachment 1, 02 Apr 2026) and the Insignia and Markings document (Attachment 2) can be distributed to vendors who were unable to attend the 15 April site visit, so that we may submit a technically responsive quote.

Thank you again for your time. We are committed to delivering a fully assembled, conforming product by 03 August 2026 and welcome any additional guidance.
