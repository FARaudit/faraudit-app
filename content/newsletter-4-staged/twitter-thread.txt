[1/5] Most founders lose 6-7 figures of net worth before Series A — and never see it happen.

The cause: one missed IRS form. The 83(b) election. 30 days from stock purchase. No extensions.

What just cost four of my friends their first exit 🧵

---

[2/5] How it works:

Buy founder stock at par ($0.0001/sh). IRS sees zero income.

But your stock vests over 4 yrs. Default IRS treatment: tax you on FMV at each vest event.

Year 2 vest worth $1M? You owe ordinary income on $1M — even though you can't sell to pay the bill.

---

[3/5] 83(b) flips it.

You tell the IRS: "Recognize the income now — when it's zero. Don't tax me at vesting."

All future appreciation = long-term capital gains.

On a $50M exit, 37% ordinary vs 20% LTCG on the bulk of your equity = keeping your house vs selling it.

---

[4/5] The deadline:

30 days from your stock purchase date. Not incorporation. Not the board resolution. The day the SPA was executed.

IRS doesn't care that:
— you didn't know
— your lawyer "was going to handle it"
— your CPA was on vacation

Miss the window, miss it forever.
