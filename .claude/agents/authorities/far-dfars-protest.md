# Authority Pack — FAR/DFARS structure, clauses, protest grounds, contract types
Verified against .gov primary sources, as-of 2026-06-21. Confirm volatile items live before quoting.

## FAR Overhaul (EO 14275, "Restoring Common Sense to Federal Procurement," signed 2025-04-15) — IN FLUX
- First comprehensive FAR rewrite; led by OFPP + FAR Council. Goal: return FAR to statutory roots, plain language, remove most non-statutory rules. [acquisition.gov/far-overhaul]
- Mechanism is MODEL DEVIATION TEXT, not yet a final rule: the Council issues plain-language replacement text part-by-part; agencies adopt via their own class deviations (~30 days). Formal rulemaking begins only after all parts are issued. [acquisition.gov/far-overhaul/faqs]
- Model deviation text observed: Parts 15,16,22,23,25,32,42,53 (2025-09-30); updated Parts 2,52 (2025-10-28). Ongoing.
- IMPLICATION: NOT a renumbering (that claim is UNVERIFIED). What IS verified: the operative clause set for a solicitation now depends on the AGENCY + DATE — the deviation text can differ from the still-published official FAR. Treat the live FAR text + the agency's applicable deviation as the controlling pair. Verify per solicitation.

## Uniform Contract Format (FAR 15.204-1, Table 15-1)
- Part I Schedule: A form · B supplies/services + prices/costs · C description/specs/SOW · D packaging/marking · E inspection/acceptance · F deliveries/performance · G contract admin data · H special contract requirements.
- Part II: I contract clauses.
- Part III: J list of attachments.
- Part IV Reps & Instructions: K reps/certs · L instructions to offerors · M evaluation factors for award. (Part IV retained in file, not physically in awarded contract.) [acquisition.gov/far/15.204-1, /15.204-2]

## FAR 52.252-2 Clauses Incorporated by Reference
- Incorporated clauses have "the same force and effect as if given in full text"; CO provides full text on request. [acquisition.gov/far/52.252-2]
- Reading rule: a clause list is often just numbers/titles/dates — resolve each incorporated cite to its full text (and any agency deviation version) to capture the real requirements.

## GAO Bid Protests (4 C.F.R. Part 21)
- Protest = challenge to an award/proposed award OR to the TERMS of a solicitation. [gao.gov/legal/bid-protests/faqs]
- TIMELINESS (4 CFR 21.2) — LOAD-BEARING: a protest of solicitation terms/improprieties must be filed BEFORE the time set for receipt of initial proposals; other protests within 10 days of knowing the basis. This codifies the patent-ambiguity doctrine — a PATENT (apparent on the face) defect must be raised PRE-BID or it is waived; a LATENT ambiguity may be raised later.
- Common grounds: defective/ambiguous solicitation; unduly restrictive requirements; undisclosed/unstated evaluation criteria; improper set-aside; OCI. Dismissal grounds at 4 CFR 21.5. [gao.gov/assets/gao-18-510sp.pdf]
- OCI — FAR Subpart 9.5: three categories — unequal access to non-public info; biased ground rules (drafted the specs); impaired objectivity (evaluating own work). Disclose; CO may avoid/neutralize/mitigate/waive. [acquisition.gov/far/part-9]

## FAR Part 16 Contract Types
- FFP (16.2): price not adjustable for cost experience; contractor bears full cost risk.
- Cost-reimbursement (16.3): pays allowable incurred costs up to an estimated-cost ceiling not exceeded without CO approval.
- T&M / Labor-Hour: expressly NOT fixed-price; used when extent/duration can't be estimated at award.
- IDIQ (16.5): indefinite quantity within min/max over a fixed period; work via task/delivery orders. [acquisition.gov/far/part-16]
