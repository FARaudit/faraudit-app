# Authority Pack — Small-Business Eligibility
Verified against .gov sources, as-of 2026-06-21. Never assert $/employee thresholds from memory — use the SBA lookup live.

## Size Standards (13 CFR Part 121)
- Keyed to NAICS; based on average annual receipts ($) OR employee count, by industry. [sba.gov/federal-contracting/contracting-guide/size-standards]
- Look up current standard via the Size Standards Tool (sba.gov/size-standards) or the Table of Size Standards. Regulatory text = 13 CFR Part 121.
- Receipts averaging period for federal contracting = latest 5 complete fiscal years.
- DO NOT quote a specific threshold without a live lookup + citation.

## Set-aside programs + current certification authority
- 8(a) Business Development — socially/economically disadvantaged; certified by SBA (MySBA Certifications).
- SDVOSB/VOSB — certification moved from VA to SBA effective 2023-01-01 (NDAA FY2021); now SBA VetCert. [sba.gov; veterans.certify.sba.gov]
- HUBZone — historically underutilized business zones; certified by SBA.
- WOSB/EDWOSB — certified by SBA (MySBA; approved third-party certifiers also recognized).

## Limitations on Subcontracting (FAR 52.219-14 / 13 CFR 125.6)
Prime may not pay MORE than the following to subs that are NOT similarly situated (% of the amount paid by the Government):
- Services (except construction): 50%
- Supplies (other than nonmanufacturer): 50%, excluding cost of materials
- General construction: 85%, excluding cost of materials
- Special trade construction: 75%, excluding cost of materials
- Similarly situated entity = first-tier sub with the SAME small-business program status as the prime AND small under the subcontract's NAICS; its work counts toward the prime's self-performance. [acquisition.gov/far/52.219-14]

## Nonmanufacturer Rule (13 CFR 121.406)
- A non-manufacturer can qualify as small on a supply set-aside if: <=500 employees, primarily retail/wholesale, takes ownership/possession per industry practice, and supplies the product of a small U.S. manufacturer. SBA may waive (class or individual). [sba.gov; ecfr 13 CFR 121.406]

## Affiliation (13 CFR 121.103) + JV / Mentor-Protege
- Affiliation turns on CONTROL (or power to control; or a third party controlling both); affiliates' sizes combine.
- Ostensible-subcontractor rule: a non-similarly-situated sub performing the PRIMARY AND VITAL requirements, or on which the prime is unusually reliant, is treated as a JV partner -> affiliation.
- Mentor-Protege (13 CFR 125.9): approved mentor + protege not affiliated solely due to assistance; may JV as small for set-asides the protege qualifies for.
- SDVOSB JV: veteran rules RELOCATED from 13 CFR 125 (old 125.18) to 13 CFR Part 128 (e.g., 128.402) with VetCert — FLAG: verify the current section. SDVOSB partner / protege performs >=40% of the JV work.

## SAM + Reps & Certs
- FAR 52.204-8 — Annual Reps & Certs: active SAM registrants may incorporate SAM-posted reps & certs by reference (updated within 12 months).
- FAR 52.219-1 — Small Business Program Representations: offeror represents size status under the assigned NAICS + socioeconomic status; misrepresentation carries criminal/administrative penalties.
- Active SAM.gov registration is a prerequisite for award.
